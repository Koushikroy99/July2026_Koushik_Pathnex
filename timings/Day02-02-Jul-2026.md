### 📸 AWS Account Setup Screenshots (Step-by-Step)

---

#### Phase 1: Sign Up & Verify Email

#### 1. AWS Sign Up Page
Open the AWS sign up page.
![AWS Sign Up Page](../screenshots/AWS-Sign-up-page.png)

#### 2. Account Name & Email
Enter your email address in the Root user email address field. Enter an AWS account name (`koushik_roy_aws`). Click **Verify email address**.
![Account Name and Email](../screenshots/Sign-up-form-with-email-and-account-name-fields.png)

#### 3. Email Verification Code
Check your email inbox for a verification code from AWS. Enter the verification code on the AWS sign-up page.
![Email Verification Code](../screenshots/Email-verification-code-entry-screen.png)

#### 4. Create Root User Password
Type in a strong password for your root user. Confirm the password by typing it again. Click **Continue**.
> 💡 **Pro tip:** Use a password manager like 1Password or Bitwarden to generate and store strong passwords.
![Create Password](../screenshots/Password-creation-screen.png)

---

#### Phase 2: Choose Plan & Enter Contact Info

**5. Choose Account Plan**
Select **Choose free plan**. The Free plan gives you 6 months of access with up to $200 in credits.
![Choose Account Plan](../screenshots/choose-your-account-plan-page-with-free-and-paid-options.png)

---

**6. Contact Information**
Type in your full name (`Koushik Roy`), phone number, and location information (India, Kolkata). Read the AWS Customer Agreement, tick the checkbox, and click **Agree and Continue**.
![Contact Information](../screenshots/contact-information-fields-for-full-name-phone-number-and-address.png)

---

**7. Billing Information**
On the Billing Information page, enter your credit/debit card information. You will not get charged (only a temporary $1 hold for verification).
![Billing Information](../screenshots/billing-information-form-with-credit-card-fields.png)

---

**8. Payment Method**
Confirm your billing address and payment method. You might be redirected to your bank's website to authorize the verification charge.
![Payment Method](../screenshots/payment-method-entry-form.png)

---

#### Phase 3: Confirm Identity & Support Plan

**9. Phone Verification**
Select your Country code from the dropdown (India +91). Enter your mobile phone number. Click **Send SMS**.
![Phone Verification](../screenshots/confirm-your-identity-page-with-country-code-and-phone-number-fields.png)

---

**10. SMS Code Entry**
Enter the verification code you received via text message. Click **Continue**.
![SMS Code Entry](../screenshots/verification-code-entry-screen.png)

---

**11. Support Plan Selection**
On the Select a support plan page, choose **Basic support - Free**. Click **Complete sign up**.
![Support Plan - Basic](../screenshots/support-plan-selection-page-with-basic-plan-highlighted.png)

---

**12. Free Plan Eligibility Notice (Optional)**
If you see a message saying "You are not eligible for the free plan", it means your information is associated with an existing AWS account. You will be upgraded to a paid plan (pay-as-you-go). Check the acknowledgment box and click **Confirm** to continue.
![Not Eligible for Free Plan](../screenshots/you-are-not-eligible-for-the-free-plan-message.png)

---

#### Phase 4: Log in to AWS Console

**13. Root Sign In - Email**
Open the AWS Console. Enter your email address in the Root user email address field. Click **Next**.
![Root Sign In Email](../screenshots/Root-user-sign-in-form-Email.png)

---

**14. Root Sign In - Password**
Enter your root user password. Click **Sign in**.
![Root Sign In Password](../screenshots/Root-user-sign-in-form-Password.png)

---

**15. AWS Management Console Dashboard**
You are now logged in! You can see your Account Name (`koushik_roy_aws`) and Account ID in the top right corner.
![Dashboard and Account Name](../screenshots/Account-name-in-AWS-Management-Console.png)

---

#### Phase 5: Enable Free Tier Usage Alerts

**16. Billing Menu**
In the top right corner of the AWS Management Console, click your account name. Select **Billing and Cost Management** from the dropdown menu.
![Billing Menu](../screenshots/Billing-and-Cost-Management-menu-item.png)

---

**17. Billing Preferences**
Click **Billing preferences** in the left sidebar.
![Billing Preferences](../screenshots/Billing-Preferences-in-Preferences-and-Settings-section.png)

---

**18. Edit Alert Preferences**
Scroll down to the Alert preferences section. Click **Edit**.
![Edit Alert Preferences](../screenshots/Alert-preferences-sectionwithEditbutton-highlighted.png)

---

**19. Enable Alerts**
Check the box next to **Receive AWS Free Tier alerts**. (Optionally, enter a different email address). Click **Update**.
> 💡 AWS will send you an email when your usage reaches 85% of a Free Tier limit to protect you from surprise bills.
![Enable Alerts](../screenshots/Receive-AWS-FreeTieralertscheckboxenabledwithemailandCloudWatch-options.png)
